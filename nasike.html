RIGHTEOUS REBELLION

NASIKE'S FIGHT AGAINST FEMALE GENITAL MUTILATION

Nasike Legupa is a 10-year-old girl from Samburu County in Northern Kenya. She lost both parents when she was still a little girl with the mother dying first followed by the step-father a few years later.

Her life has been, in her words, “a bird’s life. … you see a bird doesn’t know what it will eat and probably not where it will sleep on any single day and sometimes it has to perch and even sleep on a thorny shrub” She means having parents or not is still the same for her as she quips. Why so? Nasike’s mother was mentally ill when Nasike was born. So she took no care of her and her step-father on the other hand didn’t care since it is not the work of Turkana men to take care of little and even older children! That is women’s work! In her early childhood, life was very difficult for her. She depended on the goodwill of relatives and friends to survive. In the circumstances, she did not even join school when she attained school going age.

There was nobody to enroll her in school nor get her the mere basics like school uniform, registration fees and books, let alone other girl’s needs.

The height of frustration came when one day, just before dawn, her father came home with a group of old women and some older men whose agenda was clear to her. The women were on mission to carry out Female Genital Mutilation on her while one of the old men in tow would immediately declare her an adult after the cut and therefore, his wife! She shivered with fear and trepidation. Thinking quickly, she slithered through a space in the walls of her hut and vanished into the darkness never to return. Her athletic legs helped her to outrun her pursuers as the elders went after baying for her blood. All that trailed her were shrill shouts of bloody-over thirty women and men pursuing but they were no match for her. They lost and she won this first battle. Later she leant that her father died. Perhaps he could not bear the frustration of losing dowry after Nasike escaped, and the shame of being branded a father of a coward!

Before this incident, Nasike had been hearing from other girls that life was good, “in the big city, that had something called electricity, a lot of water and fashionable things’, that every girl fancied. The city they referred to is, Samburu county headquarters Maralal. She had been misled to believe that she could get work as a house help. She walked for days through dangerous wilderness asking for directions to the city and finally got to her destination without any idea where to start, what to drink or eat.

She had no idea that it was illegal for anyone to employ a minor as that would be child-labor. So she didn’t get what brought her, a job as a house help! The, “big city”, wasn’t as rosy as painted by her age mates. Whereas community back in the village seemed to care and she could get food and a place to sleep in any household she walked into, in town life was different. People were indifferent, mean and exploitative. Some adults even attempted to molest her sexually and she had to keep ducking them in the dark and cold streets where she slept in the company of other street children.

For some time, she lived here but she didn’t quite get used as a result of the many dangers that larked in the shadows, especially attempts by fellow male children to gang rape her and other girls. Together with other children, they survived on begging in the streets and rummaging food waste from dust bins. Luckily, small eateries owned by her tribesmen gave them left over food and this helped them to survive.

Help came in disguise when one day, in the wee hours of the morning, the local police raided their hideout and took them away, into a local recue center run by the Catholic Church Sisters known as Suguta Girls Rescue Centre Centre in Samburu. This became her new home where there was sanity, caring Sisters (nuns), a place to sleep and food.

Here she was also happy to learn abit of Kiswahili (National language) through the help of the Catholic sisters. This helped her be able to communicate with other people. But she still, missed a family she could identify with. The Rescue Center is where Shared Values Foundation team met Nasike Legupae during a familiarization tour of the County. We had visited the Rescue Center because the center is the run-to as well as receiving center for girls running away from female genital mutilation. We had visited the Rescue Center because the center is the run-to as well as receiving center for girls running away from female genital mutilation.

The first girl we met was Nasike Legupae who came running to us into the warm hug of our team lead, Ms. Lucy Kathurima. The hug lasted what appeared like an eternity as the girl could not let go off the visitor. When she finally loosed her grip and looked Lucy in the face, her first words, heavy with yearning, were, “mom, buy me clothes for Christmas so that I look like other lucky girls.” The next staccato sentence was, “please take me to school”. She meant that she yearned to join a school as the Rescue Center was for her, ‘a sort of prison’,.

The Centre didn’t have a school and it was overcrowded. But the nuns were surely doing what had to be done to get the street and gang life out of the girls, and mentor Female Genital Mutilation survivors... After Nasike regained herself from what appeared like a torpor, we sat to hear her story above, told between breaks of sobs and tears.

Later we spoke to many of her rescue center-mates and their stories were heart rending as some had actually been forcefully cut against their will and married off even before healing their wounds, some were forced to consummate their forced marriages while still bleeding from their fresh cuts; some with young babies; while the unlucky-lucky ones were banished from their homes, family and community for, “defying the elders and shaming their community by their cowardly acts!” After the long day of hearing depressing stories of cruelty against children in the name of culture, it was time to leave.

We promised Nasike that we would take her to a nice boarding school with other beautiful girls , beautiful school uniform, building like those of the city she dreamt of and caring parents like the kind nuns who rescued her from the madness of the town streets. But we made it clear that we had to get permission from her current parents (meaning the nuns in charge of the center) who recommended that we take her to a school close to us so that we can be paying her visits. We took her a school managed by the same Mary Immaculate congregation. When we returned to Nairobi, SVF mobilized resources and secured Nasike a place in Mary Immaculate Girls Boarding school in Mweiga, Nyeri. Since she only had little knowledge of Kiswahili and English acquired from Mary Immaculate Rescue Centre, she could only start at grade 2 despite her age of 10 years, which was overage for grade 2.

Her joy of wearing a new uniform – the only new (not second hand clothes) she had ever worn in her life and joining a new, “big” school was the greatest joy of her life….

And SVF’s greatest joy too. Follow-up by SVF, which continues to support her school fees and personal needs, shows that Nasike settled down very fast and is a well behaved girl having received love and acceptance from a school she calls home. What isn’t more, she is doing well in school.

She aspires to perform well in school, advance her studies and become a lawyer. Your guess is as good as ours why she wants to be a lawyer. From then on, Nasike Legupae has become SVF’s symbol of our determination to fight the FGM scourge and to lend a hand to victims of this retrogressive cultural practice and other girls in difficult circumstances like Nasike Legupae.

Other girls left at Suguta Girls Rescue Centre Center are also equally in our mind as SVF and we mobilize resources to support as many as we can as they also need clothing, food, fees and personal effects and parental love. Remember, almost all of them are neglected by their families for defying parents, community and cultural requirement to be, “made women and good wives”, through the cut. Join us to support Nasike Legupae and other Mary Rescue Center and out in other 21 FGM hot spots in Kenya where girls and women are faced with the threat of Female Genital Mutilation.
