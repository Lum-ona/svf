    Who We Are

    Founded in 2014, Shared Values Foundation (SVF) is a Kenyan NGO inspired by global sustainable development principles outlined in the Social Responsibility ISO 26000 guidelines. Guided by our SCAPII values, we strive for equity in all our charitable endeavors, collaborating with like-minded individuals and organizations. Since inception, SVF has partnered with donors like the African Circle of Hope Foundation and the Gstaad Rotary Club to make strides in community development across Meru, Nairobi, and Muranga counties. Achievements include improving sanitation at St. Lucy’s Primary School for the Blind, distributing food to over 550 during COVID-19, sponsoring secondary education for 300 students annually, supporting 1800 girls with menstrual hygiene, and conducting medical camps benefitting 500 individuals. Additionally, SVF surveyed FGM in Samburu, engaging with survivors and planning expansion to all 21 hotspot counties. Focus areas include education, child welfare, and environmental concerns.

    Where we Work

    Shared Values Foundation’s programming mandate includes 19 counties (click here) of Kenya, of which 12 are Arid and Semi-Arid Lands (ASALs) and 7 non-ASAL (click –counties). We have initially done some work in Nairobi, Muranga and Meru and getting our foot prints in Isiolo and Samburu counties, eventually planning to reach Marsabit and concentrate in this cluster; hoping to venture into the other counties as we grow and raise the necessary resources. Our entry into the listed counties is informed by overwhelming needs of their populations.

    The counties, especially the ASAL one, are characterized by general lack of natural resources and are perennially beset with food and nutrition-poverty owing to encyclical droughts which often wipe out their one stream source of livelihood; livestock and have to start all over again when rains return that are also short-lived and the cycle is repeated.

    The non-ASAL counties are on the other hand characterized by good weather, adequate natural resources that support their livelihoods but they also have pockets of extremely poor rural (and urban slum) folks who are perpetually in need of relief food during periods of drought, hence experiencing occasional cycles of food insecurity.

    In both cases, poverty in turn affects the participation of children, youth and women in education, social and economic life of their country. Our choice of the counties, therefore and the proposed interventions are aimed at empowering the populations and ensuring that they access the basic services and rights so they can develop themselves, give their children a better future and be able to contribute to national development.

    We therefore, work in rural and slum areas with the down and middle stream populations where there is prevalence of educational, social and economic inequality to bridge the resource, knowledge and skill gaps.

    Community Development Issues

    The world over Kenya included, children, youth and women are the section of population often left behind socially and economically and more vulnerable to social injustices, internal conflicts, impacts of climate change, diseases such as HIV/AIDS and COVID- knowledge19, knowledge and skills gaps.

    This is pushing more and more of the vulnerable populations into poverty and retrogressive activities such as human trafficking, prostitution, and crime for survival. Phenomenal increase in cases of mental illnesses and suicides is also attributed to these challenges.

    For girls and young women, coercion into inhuman cultural practices like early marriages, FGM and child labour which are common in several northern Kenya cultures is still a major concern of our times.

    Shared Values Foundation has taken up the challenge to attempt to address these issues by dealing primarily with the root causes as well as the impacts, aiming to empower them by equipping them with the right knowledge, skills, attitudes and the wherewithal to confront their challenges, achieve a turnaround, particularly in their livelihoods and become active and productive participants in their own development and that of our country.

    Our Approach

    Shared Values Foundation employs an inclusive and integrated approach to facilitate programs that lead to no poverty, zero hunger, promotion of good health and wellbeing and quality education, having clean water and sanitation in the communities, promotion of decent work and economic growth, reduced inequalities, climate action initiatives, promotion of peaceful, just and strong institutions and enabling partnership and collaboration through shared values. The approaches include community mobilization and animation, supporting formal and non-formal education for children and youth while promoting basic literacy and numeracy in the adult populations including digital knowledge and skills and financial literacy, support development and implementation of community driven income generating initiatives, support climate action programs including knowledge on climate mitigation and adaptation, and small enterprises particularly in agribusiness among others.

    Programmes and Interventions.

    Faithful to our core values, Shared Values Foundation’s programs are geared to supporting the humanity, good causes and the realization of Sustainable Development Goals; particularly, SDG’s 1, 3. 4, 5, 6, 8 and 13, through our work with Kenyan communities. Our route to programing is what we call Pathways to Prosperity (P2P) which encompass quality and equitable Education, basic health and well-being, gender, safe water and sanitation, environmental health and climate action, and socio-economic empowerment programmes.

    • 01 Quality and Equitable Education.

      Shared Values Foundation focuses on providing equitable access to quality education for vulnerable children, collaborating with local government and the Ministry of Education, addressing barriers through support services like learning materials and psychosocial support, community mobilization to challenge cultural practices, and partnering with others in the sector to facilitate innovation and scale, aiming to support children and youth through formal, non-formal, and vocational pathways towards sustainable livelihoods.

    • 02 Basic Health and Wellbeing.

      Communities facing severe health challenges due to lack of access to basic services and cultural barriers are supported by Shared Values Foundation through partnerships and initiatives addressing issues like malnutrition, cultural practices, and preventive healthcare, aiming for sustainable impact.

    • 03 Socio-Economic Empowerment

      SVF addresses agricultural and pastoralist communities' vulnerabilities to climate change in eight target counties through sustainable livelihood programs, including training in modern farming techniques, access to capital, climate change adaptation, and market linkages, fostering resilience and economic empowerment.
